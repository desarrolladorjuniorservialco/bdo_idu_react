# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Field operatives, site supervisors, inspectors (interventoría), supervision managers, and admins working on a public infrastructure construction contract in Bogotá, Colombia. Context is mixed: office desktops for end-of-day logging and reporting, mobile and tablet for field checks during site visits. Users are technically proficient in their domain (construction, project management) but not necessarily tech-savvy. The primary task on any screen is recording, reviewing, or approving evidence of construction progress for a legally binding government contract.

## Product Purpose

BOB (Sistema de Bitácora Digital de Obra) is the digital field diary for Servialco's BDO-IDU-1556-2025 contract (Grupo 4) with the IDU (Instituto de Desarrollo Urbano), Bogotá. It replaces paper-based logbooks with a structured, traceable system for daily annotations, budget tracking, correspondence, PMT follow-up, environmental and social components, weekly closings, and PDF report generation. Success means field teams spend less time on paperwork and more on execution, and that the resulting records hold up as legal evidence of contract compliance.

## Brand Personality

Preciso · Técnico · Eficiente. The tone is institutional but not bureaucratic — like a well-designed civil engineering instrument rather than a government form. Information density is a feature, not a flaw. Users trust the tool because it behaves predictably, presents data clearly, and gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS blue-and-white: the startup playbook (hero gradient, identical card grid, teal CTA). BOB is a professional tool, not a product marketing page.
- Legacy government software: gray tables, Arial, white backgrounds, forms that feel like 2005 Windows applications. BOB should feel like it was made in the current decade.

## Design Principles

1. **Density as respect** — Show users what they need without forcing unnecessary progressive disclosure. Field professionals can handle information-rich screens; hiding data behind extra taps wastes their time.
2. **Evidence-grade reliability** — Visual consistency and predictable behavior matter because the output (reports, logs) is legal documentation. Anything that looks broken erodes trust in the record.
3. **Technical, not decorative** — Decorative elements only earn their place if they reinforce hierarchy or orientation. Backgrounds, animations, and color accents must serve the data, not the other way around.
4. **Mixed-context readiness** — Layouts and touch targets must work on a field tablet under direct sunlight as well as a site office laptop. Assume the user is in a hurry.
5. **Institutional precision over generic freshness** — The alternative to old government software is not generic SaaS. It is something that looks like it was designed for this specific industry: precise, measured, and credible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Mixed desktop and mobile use; some field use on tablets. Maintain sufficient contrast ratios for outdoor legibility (aim for AAA on critical status indicators). Touch targets minimum 44×44px for mobile screens. Reduced motion preference is already implemented and must be preserved.
